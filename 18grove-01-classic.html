Eighteen Grove · Coconut Grove, Miami

Eighteen Grove was founded by an operator who helped his own family hand over a business they had run for fifty years — someone who knows what it is to sit across the table wondering whether the people who built something will be looked after once the founder is gone. Before founding Eighteen Grove, he led more than thirty transactions across Facility Services, Specialty Distribution, Insurance Services, and QSR, and held senior finance and corporate-development roles at private-equity-backed platforms in Facility Services and Distribution. Ownership TransitionsGuideJune 2026

Recap, partial sale, or full exit: a founder's guide to ownership transitions

Most founder- and family-owned businesses reach a point where the owner begins to think about what comes next. The good news is that "selling" is not a single decision — it is a spectrum, and the right point on it depends on what you want for yourself, your family, and your team.

The three paths

A full sale. You sell the business outright and step away on a timeline you choose. It is the cleanest exit, and the right one when you are ready to fully retire or move on. A good buyer keeps your management, protects the name, and gives your people continuity.

A partial sale or recapitalization. You sell a portion of the business and roll the rest into the next chapter — taking meaningful chips off the table while keeping equity, and upside, in what comes next. This suits owners who want liquidity but are not done building.

A value-added partnership. You stay at the helm and bring on a partner for capital, experience, and support. The goal is growth, not exit; you remain the driver.

How to choose

The right path usually comes down to three questions: How much do you want to step back — now, versus over time? How important is keeping equity in the future of the business? And whom do you trust to be the next steward of what you have built? Price matters, but for most owners we meet, it is rarely the first thing, or the only thing.

A note on timing

There is no wrong time to have a confidential, no-obligation conversation. Understanding your options early — even years before you act — almost always leads to a better outcome than waiting until you are forced to move.

Eighteen Grove partners with founder- and family-owned businesses across services and distribution. If you are weighing what comes next, we are glad to talk it through — privately, and with no expectation.
