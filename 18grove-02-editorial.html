Approach

A partner for
whatever comes
next.

We are a partner for owners and their teams — to transition ownership, bring on a value-added partner, or find the right next steward.

01
Step one

A confidential first call

We talk, privately. We'll sign an NDA on request before you share anything sensitive, and nothing leaves the room without your say-so. No bankers required to say hello.

02
Step two

An indication of value — no strings

Within a few weeks we give you an honest read on value. No obligation, and no exclusivity to start. If it's not a fit, you've lost nothing.

03
Step three

You choose the path

Full sale, partial sale, or a value-added partnership where you keep building. Your team hears of any change from you, not from us.

04
Step four

Certainty of close

You deal directly with the founder — the decision-maker, not a committee. Management stays, your people keep their jobs, and we don't retrade absent a material, undisclosed problem.

Founder's NoteCoconut Grove · 2026Eighteen Grove

On continuing what owners built.

Having sold my own family's business, I know what the choice of partner means to the people who stay. This is the firm I wanted to exist.

By the firm's founderCoconut Grove · 2026
Photograph — to be commissioned
In this note
I. The owner's chair II. Why people matter III. How we partner IV. What we ask

I have sat in the owner's chair. I helped sell my family's 50-year-old business — and learned how much the choice of partner matters, not to the seller, but to everyone who stays. Eighteen Grove exists because I wanted that partner to exist.

The best businesses, in my experience, are built quietly, over decades, by owners and operators who prioritize their people and their craft. They are not the loudest companies, and they are rarely the ones for sale at auction. They are the ones whose teams have been there for years and whose customers come back without being asked.

Why people matter

When an owner sells, the numbers get all the attention. But the thing that made the business worth buying — the culture, the people, the relationships — is exactly the thing most easily broken by a new owner in a hurry. We treat that culture as the asset it is.

“We value the experience of our partners and seek to preserve their heritages while accelerating growth together.”

That is not sentiment; it is strategy. Preserving what works is how a business keeps its customers and its team through a transition — and how it keeps growing afterward.

How we partner

We invest in and actively grow founder- and family-owned businesses with existing management. Sometimes that means a full transition for an owner ready to retire; sometimes a value-added partnership for one who wants to keep building; sometimes simply being the right next steward. We keep management, work hands-on, and take a long-term view that preserves and enhances equity value.

A note: Eighteen Grove is newly launched. The figures on this site describe the businesses we seek to partner with — not a track record we are claiming. Our criteria are real; our portfolio is being built.

What we ask of an owner is simple: the chance to be a good next chapter. If you have built something worth continuing, I would like to hear about it.
