Ownership Transitions
Guide
June 2026

Recap, partial sale, or full exit

Most founder- and family-owned businesses reach a point where the owner starts to think about what comes next. "Selling" isn't a single decision — it's a spectrum, and the right point on it depends on what you want for yourself, your family, and your team.

The three paths

A full sale. You sell outright and step away on a timeline you choose — the cleanest exit, right when you're ready to fully retire. A good buyer keeps your management, protects the name, and gives your people continuity.

A partial sale or recapitalization. You sell a portion and roll the rest into the next chapter — chips off the table while keeping equity, and upside, in what comes next.

A value-added partnership. You stay at the helm and bring on a partner for capital, experience, and support. The goal is growth, not exit.

How to choose

It usually comes down to three questions: how much do you want to step back, now versus over time; how important is keeping equity in the future of the business; and whom do you trust to be the next steward. Price matters — but it's rarely the first thing, or the only thing.
